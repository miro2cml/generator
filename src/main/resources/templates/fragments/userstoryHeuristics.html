Which inputs are converted?

A User Story Map consists of Cards (that are ordered in two dimensions). Therefore only such Cards are converted. All other Miro elements are ignored. The Cards which are yellow and blue are ignored because they illustrate Tasks and Activities in the User Story Map. The titles of the Cards are relevant for the mapping; their descriptions and the other fields of the Card will be ignored.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Why are my Cards not converted?

  • The Cards are yellow or blue.
  • They do not have the correct text format. Check if you use the correct format as introduced in the tutorial.
  • The User Stories are not in the title defined of the Miro Cards.

How is the title of the User Story generated?

The title is the verb concatenated with the entity of the User Story.

Why are some numbers added to the title of the User Story?

The Context Mapper tool expects unique titles for the User Stories. If there are equal titles, the miro2cml converter adds '1' at the end of the name to make sure that the conversion produce a correct CML file.

Why do some verbs have quotations marks at the beginning and at the end?

The Context Mapper knows the CRUD verbs (create, read, update, delete). Therefore they do not need quotation marks. All other verbs are between the quotations marks to mark them as a string.
